Welcome to CITY OF THE FUTURE....!

Futurist Ian Pearson has made interesting ideas about life in the near future of humanity.

What kind of environment will we live in 30 years from now? To answer this almost impossible question, Ian Pearson, a futurist, gives his thoughts on the world in 2045.

In a report that Pearson sent to construction equipment rental company Hewden, he gave his opinion on what we can expect from the construction industry in the next 30 years. According to him, in the near future, humans will thoroughly apply robotic technology and artificial intelligence, buildings will be built according to high-tech criteria. Here are Pearson's most interesting predictions:

About

Buildings will be controlled by artificial intelligence
According to him, people in buildings will be able to chat with the building and make their requests for it to do, such as changing the temperature in the room.
Super tall buildings will appear and act like mini cities
With increasing housing demand and increasingly expensive land prices, super-tall buildings will be the solution to bring affordable and medium-sized apartments to everyone. They will act as a mini city with floors dedicated to working, offices and floors for residents to live.
Building windows will be replaced with virtual reality screens
In the movie "Back to the Future II", Marty's futuristic house is equipped with a virtual reality window that can change any landscape. According to Pearson, buildings of the future will not have windows but use virtual reality screens.
Humans will use special coatings to collect solar energy
Whether this happens will depend on the development of technology to create nanoparticle-based materials, but essentially the coating can absorb and convert sunlight into energy.
Smart temperature and light will follow you as you move around the room
Heat and light will be directed at you so you can walk around the apartment and still get the amount of light and temperature you want. The report doesn't go into detail on how the technology will work, but there's quite a bit of research on improving lighting going on right now.

Construction workers will wear support frames to be able to work with heavy equipment

This technology is being researched by companies. For example, Panasonic is working on robotic suits that can make it easier for humans to carry heavy objects. The US Department of Defense is also developing an "Iron Man" suit that can assist the wearer in many things, including carrying heavy objects with ease. According to Pearson's report, construction workers will be able to wear the power frame as soon as the technology is fully developed.

Dangerous jobs will be assigned to robots

According to Pearson, in the future robots will work with humans in many fields, but they will be the main source of work in high-risk jobs.

Underwater buildings appear, floating in the air

According to Pearson, houses under water or houses floating in the air are possible but the cost will be much higher. This makes it easier for underwater dwellings to become tourist attractions, rather than for human habitation.

3D printing technology and assembly robots will allow people to build buildings at a very fast pace

According to the report, buildings will be built using 3D printing technology as soon as we can fabricate the right types of concrete and materials.

Buildings will be taller, and no elevators will be used

The tallest building in the world is currently located in Dubai called Burj Khalifa and has 163 floors. According to Pearson, a researcher of the future, thousands of people will live in one building, creating a kind of miniature city.

The buildings can be more than 20 km high, including up to 8,000 stories. Samsung believes that people will use self-driving planes to go from floor to floor because elevators are not fast and stable enough for super-tall buildings in the future.
